# Feroz Fashion - Canadian Business Registration Guide

## 1. Provincial Registration
Depending on your location, you must register your business name (if it's not just your legal name).

### Ontario (ServiceOntario)
- **Sole Proprietorship**: Register through the Ontario Business Registry.
- **Cost**: ~$60 for 5 years.
- **Search**: Perform an Ontario Name Search (NUANS) before registering.

### British Columbia (BC Registries)
- **Sole Proprietorship**: Request a Name Approval first, then register.
- **Cost**: ~$30 (Name Request) + ~$40 (Registration).

## 2. Federal Accounts (CRA)
Register for a **Business Number (BN)** via the CRA's Business Registration Online (BRO) service.
- **GST/HST Account**: Required if your worldwide taxable supplies exceed $30,000 in a single calendar quarter or over four consecutive quarters.
- **Import/Export Program Account**: Essential if you are importing latex garments from international suppliers.

## 3. CA Identification Number
To sell textiles in Canada, you should obtain a **CA Number** from the Competition Bureau.
- **Purpose**: Identifies the "dealer" on the garment label.
- **Benefit**: Allows you to use the CA Number on labels instead of your full company name and address, preserving a cleaner look for luxury niche products.
- **Application**: Available through the Competition Bureau's website. Fee: $100 CAD.

## 4. Textile Labelling Requirements
Under the Textile Labelling Act, your latex garments must have:
- **Fiber Content**: Expressed in percentages (e.g., "100% Natural Latex").
- **Dealer Identity**: Your CA Number or full name and address.
- **Country of Origin**: (Recommended, often required for customs).
- **Care Labels**: While not strictly mandatory by law (voluntary), they are highly recommended for niche products like latex.
